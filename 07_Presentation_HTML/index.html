Part 1 · The Problem

From Panic to Precision

January 2020 · Before COVID · Before devaluation · Before everything

01

Making Charges

Local jewellers and banks charge 1–5% craftsmanship or minting premiums that are silently absorbed by buyers.

02

Bid-Ask Spreads

The spread between buying and selling price in the Egyptian market can reach 3–8%, eroding gains before any sale.

03

Currency Illusion

Rising EGP prices mask reality — when measured in USD, real gold returns are often negative after transaction costs.

If you had invested 100,000 EGP in 21K gold in January 2020 — after all costs — it would be worth 500,000+ EGP today. But why? How much was real gold appreciation vs. EGP collapse vs. local panic premium? The average Egyptian investor doesn't have the tools to answer that. We built those tools.

Part 1 · The Problem

The Core Problem

Three forces drive one price — most investors see none of them

A

Global Spot Price Movement

International gold (XAU/USD) fluctuates with Fed policy, geopolitical risk, and institutional demand — a signal most Egyptians never isolate.

B

EGP Devaluation

Three major devaluations since 2022 inflated the EGP-denominated price, creating the illusion of profit.

C

Local Panic Premium

During crises, domestic demand surges push local prices above fair value — a speculative bubble hidden in plain sight.

Part 6 · Conclusions

Key Insights

Three research findings that reframe how Egyptian investors should think about gold

💡

Finding 1 — Currency Dominance

More than half of the gold price increase in Egypt since 2020 is attributable to EGP collapse — not gold appreciation. Egyptian gold is effectively a bet on the Egyptian pound.

📈

Finding 2 — Karat Efficiency

After accounting for all real transaction costs, 21K gold delivered the best net return for individual investors — not 24K as conventional wisdom suggests. Total cost structure matters more than purity.

🎯

Finding 3 — The Right Question

Investors ask "Where is the gold price?" — the more important question is "Where is the EGP exchange rate?" Any gold purchase decision must start with an FX view.

Buying at peak premiums during crises · Ignoring transaction costs · Confusing currency devaluation with real gold gains · Following social media rumours

✅ After — Data-Driven
